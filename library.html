Tarot Card Library — Frequently Asked Questions

The 22 Major Arcana represent significant life themes and archetypes. They are: The Fool (0), The Magician (I), The High Priestess (II), The Empress (III), The Emperor (IV), The Hierophant (V), The Lovers (VI), The Chariot (VII), Strength (VIII), The Hermit (IX), Wheel of Fortune (X), Justice (XI), The Hanged Man (XII), Death (XIII), Temperance (XIV), The Devil (XV), The Tower (XVI), The Star (XVII), The Moon (XVIII), The Sun (XIX), Judgement (XX), and The World (XXI).

The Minor Arcana contains four suits of 14 cards each: Wands (fire element — creativity, passion, and ambition), Cups (water element — emotions, intuition, and relationships), Swords (air element — intellect, conflict, and truth), and Pentacles (earth element — material matters, career, and finances). Each suit runs from Ace through 10, plus Page, Knight, Queen, and King court cards.

A reversed tarot card appears upside-down in a spread and typically signals blocked, internalized, or delayed energy compared to its upright meaning. It may indicate inner work needed, resistance to a lesson, external obstacles, or the shadow side of the card. Not all readers use reversals — some prefer to read all cards upright and interpret the surrounding cards for nuance.

A standard tarot deck contains 78 cards: 22 Major Arcana and 56 Minor Arcana. The Minor Arcana is divided into four suits (Wands, Cups, Swords, Pentacles) of 14 cards each, consisting of Ace through 10 (10 pip cards) and four court cards (Page, Knight, Queen, King). This structure is consistent across most traditional tarot decks, though artwork varies widely.

The Major Arcana (22 cards, numbered 0-XXI) represent significant life events, spiritual lessons, and universal forces that shape our journey. They carry more weight in a reading. The Minor Arcana (56 cards across four suits) represent the day-to-day situations, emotions, thoughts, and material concerns we navigate. Together they provide a complete picture of human experience.

All Major Arcana cards carry significant weight, but cards like The World (completion), The Wheel of Fortune (destiny), The Tower (upheaval), Death (transformation), and The Sun (joy and success) are often considered among the most impactful. The Aces of each Minor Arcana suit also carry potent energy as seeds of new beginnings in their respective areas.
